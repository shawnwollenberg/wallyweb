# WallyWeb Blog and Mentorship Notes

These notes capture future writing and mentorship directions without publishing full articles yet.

## Direction

WallyWeb should include a blog focused on the positive use of AI, professional judgment, and mentorship for young people. The voice should come from lived experience: 25+ years building systems, watching projects fail, scaling teams, seeing startups implode, navigating enterprise politics, handling security incidents, correcting bad architecture decisions, managing customer disasters, and recovering from production outages.

The central idea: do not just teach young builders technology. Teach them how professionals think.

Most people can learn React, Python, Kubernetes, Claude Code, or future AI tools from public tutorials. The harder lessons are:

- Making decisions with incomplete information
- Earning trust
- Communicating with executives
- Managing risk
- Breaking down hard problems
- Recovering from failure
- Leading people

Those skills should become more valuable as AI improves.

## Five Pillars

1. Learn how businesses make money

Teach revenue, costs, margins, customer acquisition, retention, and sales. Engineers who understand business become much harder to replace.

2. Become an AI amplifier

Teach prompting, agent workflows, automation, verification, and human oversight. The future is humans using AI replacing humans who do not use AI.

3. Build things

Emphasize real projects rather than tutorials: websites, mobile apps, AI agents, local business tools, and side businesses. Shipping teaches adaptability.

4. Learn communication

Clear writing, presenting ideas, selling a vision, and explaining tradeoffs may become among the most underrated skills of the next decade.

5. Build resilience

Teach that careers are not linear, failure is normal, reinvention is a skill, and learning never stops.
